# AI Adoption in Italy (2025-2026): Key Insights and Data Structures

## 1. Key Insights (Executive Summary)

*   **The "Doubling" Phenomenon:** Italy has moved from a "wait-and-see" phase to an "active implementation" phase. The jump from 8.2% to 16.4% adoption in one year (2024-2025) suggests that AI is no longer a luxury but a competitive necessity. [Source: ISTAT, "Imprese e ICT", Dec 2025]
*   **The Scale Gap:** AI adoption is currently a "David vs. Goliath" story. While 53.1% of large enterprises (>250 employees) are integrated, SMEs (10-249 employees) are at 15.7%. [Source: ISTAT, Dec 2025]
*   **Generative AI as a Gateway:** GenAI (59.1% of AI users) is acting as the entry point because of its low barrier to entry. However, "Industrial AI" like Text Mining (70.8%) is where the primary structural use currently lies. [Source: ISTAT, Dec 2025]
*   **The Skills Paradox:** Companies have the budget (market up 50% to €1.8B) and the tools (GenAI licenses at 84% in large firms), but 58.6% of firms indicate a lack of specialized skills as the main barrier. [Sources: Politecnico di Milano, Feb 2026; ISTAT, Dec 2025]

## 4. Analysis of Applications

### What is most used?
1.  **Text Analysis & GenAI:** Used for summarizing technical manuals, generating reports, and automated customer service.
2.  **Predictive Maintenance:** In Manufacturing, sensors identifying machine failure before it happens.
3.  **Quality Control:** Computer vision in Plastic/Rubber to spot defects at high speeds.

### What isn't developed yet (The 2026 "White Spaces")?
1.  **Full Supply Chain Autonomy:** AI that not only predicts shortages but automatically negotiates with suppliers (Agentic AI).
2.  **Edge AI in Small Workshops:** Bringing AI directly onto small machines without cloud dependency.
3.  **Deep Circularity:** AI that can identify the exact chemical composition of mixed plastic waste in real-time for 100% recycling.
